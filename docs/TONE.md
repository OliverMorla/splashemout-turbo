# Splash 'Em Out Tone Guide

Use this guide for site copy, navigation labels, service pages, forms, FAQs, CTAs, empty states, and local SEO content.

This file governs language and customer posture. Use [VISUAL.md](VISUAL.md) for colors, layout, typography, imagery, motion, and interface atmosphere.

## Core Voice

Splash 'Em Out should sound:

- Friendly and local.
- Clean and practical.
- Direct about price, hours, and next steps.
- Helpful without sounding overly polished.
- Confident about service quality.
- Family-friendly without becoming cute.

The voice should feel like a dependable local team that knows laundry is a chore and can make it easier.

## Positioning

Primary positioning:

> Clean, fully attended laundromats plus wash-and-fold, pickup and delivery, dry cleaning, and commercial laundry service across Central Kentucky.

Short positioning:

> Laundry day, handled.

Use supporting lines such as:

- Clean laundry without the chore.
- Find a clean laundromat near you.
- Schedule pickup and delivery in minutes.
- Big loads, small loads, we handle it all.
- Commercial laundry that keeps your business moving.

Never position Splash 'Em Out as:

- A luxury lifestyle service.
- A national faceless laundry app.
- A dry cleaner only.
- A discount-only commodity laundromat.
- A vague cleaning company without clear laundry services.

## Audience Assumption

Assume visitors are busy, local, and task-driven. They may be on a phone, searching near me, comparing prices, looking for hours, trying to wash bulky bedding, or trying to get a business quote.

Copy should answer the practical question first, then build trust.

## Copy Principles

- Lead with the customer job: find, schedule, drop off, request, call.
- Put price, turnaround, minimums, and service area near relevant CTAs.
- Use local place names naturally: Lexington, Richmond, Nicholasville, and nearby Kentucky communities.
- Distinguish drop-off service from pickup and delivery.
- Use plain service names consistently.
- Keep form labels literal and specific.
- Use phone numbers and online scheduling links where they reduce friction.
- Make commercial copy operational: volume, frequency, turnaround, laundry type, pickup and delivery.

Avoid:

- Clever copy that hides the service.
- Overly precious language about clothes.
- Generic "we care" claims without proof.
- Laundry puns in conversion-critical headings.
- Long paragraphs on mobile.
- Using "free" unless the offer is specific and true.

## Service Copy Patterns

Good:

> Drop off your laundry and our attendants will wash, dry, and fold it for pickup the next day. Wash-and-fold starts at $1.69/lb with a $15 minimum.

Good:

> Schedule pickup online, leave your laundry in the spot you choose, and we will return it clean, folded, and ready to put away within 24-48 hours.

Good:

> Request a commercial laundry bid for towels, linens, uniforms, blankets, and recurring pickup needs.

Avoid:

> Experience a revolutionary new relationship with laundry.

Avoid:

> We are the best laundry solution for everyone.

## Calls To Action

Primary CTAs should be concrete:

- Schedule Pickup
- Find a Location
- View Pricing
- Request a Bid
- Call Now
- Get Directions
- See Services
- Login

Use CTA hierarchy by intent:

- Residential convenience: Schedule Pickup.
- Local laundromat search: Find a Location.
- Commercial pages: Request a Bid.
- Pricing pages: Schedule Pickup or Find a Location.
- Mobile sticky actions: Call and Schedule.

Avoid vague CTAs:

- Learn More as the only action.
- Get Started without context.
- Discover the Difference.
- Join the Movement.

## Commercial Tone

Commercial copy should sound reliable, specific, and operational. Buyers need to know whether Splash 'Em Out can handle volume, frequency, turnaround, and pickup logistics.

Use:

- recurring pickup
- custom quote
- same-day emergency options
- next-day turnaround
- towels, linens, uniforms, blankets
- volume and frequency
- business address

Avoid:

- consumer-style emotional copy.
- vague "business solutions."
- promising exact commercial prices before quoting.

## FAQ And Error Tone

FAQs should be direct and scannable. Answer the question in the first sentence.

Errors should explain what happened and what to do next:

> Enter a phone number so we can contact you about your quote.

> Select a location before requesting directions.

Do not use apologetic or jokey errors in scheduling, quote, pricing, or contact flows.

## Success Standard

Good Splash 'Em Out copy helps someone make a decision faster. If a line does not clarify the service, price, location, trust point, or next step, cut it or move it lower on the page.
