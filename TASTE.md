I have loaded tasteskill v2 (experimental) as my only source of design rules.

Brief:

- Page kind: local service marketing landing page with strong conversion paths.
- Product: Splash 'Em Out, a Central Kentucky laundry platform for laundromats, wash-and-fold, pickup and delivery, dry cleaning pickup, and commercial laundry.
- Audience: busy local residents, families, students, near-me laundromat searchers, and business buyers who need towels, linens, uniforms, blankets, or recurring laundry handled reliably.
- Vibe words: fresh, practical, local, high-trust.
- References: modern local-service utility more than startup SaaS. Use Tailwind neutral for page structure and text, Clean Water blue `#0EA5E9` for primary actions, Deep Water `#075985` for high-contrast moments, Fresh Mint `#14B8A6` for cleanliness, and Citrus `#F59E0B` only for promotions. The page should feel as crisp as a well-run service brand like a premium car wash or modern urgent-care locator, but with warmer local laundry proof.
- Avoid: beige wellness, purple AI gradients, generic detergent bubbles, dark moody laundromat photos, luxury fashion laundry styling, fake lifestyle stock photography, hidden pricing, vague CTAs, nested card stacks, and any homepage that delays Schedule Pickup or Find a Location.

Step 1. Declare your design read in one sentence and the three dial values with one-line reasoning each. Stop.

Step 2 (after my OK). Ship a single Next.js page with at least 8 sections. Pick the sections that actually fit the product. At least 4 different layout families across the page. Use real images when available; if missing, use generated production-style imagery or clearly temporary placeholders. Lock one theme for the whole page.

Step 3. Run in writing:

- Em-dash audit: zero em-dashes U+2014 or en-dashes U+2013 anywhere.
- Pre-Flight Check: every box marked Pass or Fail with one-line justification.
- Section-Layout-Repetition audit: list each section's layout family.
- Hero discipline audit: headline lines, subtext words, CTA visibility.

Any Fail blocks completion.
