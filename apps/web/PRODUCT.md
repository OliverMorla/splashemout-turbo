# Product

## Register

local service website

## Users

Splash 'Em Out serves people in Central Kentucky who need laundry done clearly, quickly, and reliably.

Primary users:

- Local residents searching for a clean laundromat.
- Busy families and professionals who want wash-and-fold or pickup and delivery.
- Students near University of Kentucky and Eastern Kentucky University.
- People washing bulky items such as comforters, blankets, rugs, pet beds, and horse blankets.

Commercial users:

- Airbnb and VRBO hosts.
- Restaurants, bars, spas, salons, gyms, clinics, veterinary offices, hotels, universities, and equine businesses.
- Buyers who need recurring pickup, consistent turnaround, custom pricing, and a clear bid process.

## Product Purpose

The web app should make four high-intent actions easy:

- Schedule pickup and delivery.
- Find a nearby laundromat.
- View pricing.
- Request a commercial bid.

The site supports five service lines:

- Self-service laundry.
- Drop-off wash-and-fold.
- Pickup and delivery laundry.
- Commercial laundry.
- Dry cleaning pickup and delivery.

Success means customers can quickly understand where to go, what it costs, how turnaround works, and what action to take next.

## Brand Personality

Splash 'Em Out should feel clean, bright, friendly, local, dependable, and useful. It should be slightly playful in brand energy, but serious about service details.

The interface should feel like a well-run local operation, not a generic laundry marketplace.

## Anti-References

Splash 'Em Out must not look or behave like:

- A generic startup SaaS page with vague CTAs.
- A stock-photo laundromat template.
- A luxury clothing-care brand.
- A dark, gritty, unattended laundromat.
- A discount coupon site.
- A thin local SEO site with duplicate city pages.
- A commercial laundry page that makes buyers hunt for the quote form.

## Design Principles

- Location before lifestyle: local users need addresses, hours, phone numbers, directions, and nearby service options.
- Pricing before persuasion: price and minimums should be close to the service decision.
- Scheduling before storytelling: pickup and delivery needs a direct path to the external scheduling portal.
- Proof before polish: use real locations, machines, attendants, and laundry output when possible.
- Commercial clarity before sales language: quote forms should gather volume, frequency, turnaround, and laundry type.

## Accessibility And Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as a baseline with readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, and reduced-motion support.

Design for mobile users who may be walking into a laundromat, comparing prices, or trying to call while multitasking. Important actions should stay reachable and labels should be literal.
